Before You Begin

A quick refresher on the frameworks you'll apply in this simulation.

💡

Business Model Innovation

Rethinking how a company creates and captures value

Business model innovation goes beyond product improvement — it's about changing the fundamental way a company makes money. Freemium, subscriptions, ad-supported tiers, and marketplace models are all different ways to structure value capture.

How to apply it

Ask: Who pays, and for what? What happens if we change the pricing structure? Are we leaving money on the table — or are we overcharging and limiting growth? What's the trade-off between user growth and revenue per user?

Example

Spotify offers a free tier with ads and a premium tier without. Should they raise premium prices, add a cheaper tier, or push more users toward free? Each path reshapes the entire business model.

Competitive Strategy

Understanding forces that shape your market position

Porter's Five Forces helps you analyse the competitive landscape: threat of new entrants, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers, threat of substitutes, and industry rivalry. In music streaming, supplier power (record labels) is uniquely high.

How to apply it

Map the five forces for your market. Ask: Which force is strongest? Where are we most vulnerable? Can we shift a force in our favour — e.g., by reducing supplier power through exclusive content or originals?

Example

Apple enters music streaming with deep pockets and a built-in user base. Spotify must decide: compete on price, differentiate on features, or expand into new content like podcasts to reduce dependence on labels.

Strategic Acquisition

Build vs buy — knowing when to acquire capability

Strategic acquisitions accelerate a company's roadmap by buying capability, talent, or market position instead of building from scratch. The key is knowing when the time-to-market advantage of buying outweighs the integration risk.

How to apply it

Ask: Can we build this ourselves in a reasonable timeframe? What does the target give us that we can't replicate? What's the integration risk? Does this acquisition make us strategically stronger or just bigger?

Example

Spotify acquires podcast companies like Gimlet and Anchor to own exclusive content and reduce label dependence. But does owning production studios fit a platform company's DNA?

3 of 4
🎨

Product Design

Personalisation, UX, and growth through product decisions

Product design in a platform context means making decisions about personalisation algorithms, user experience, and how the product itself drives growth. Features like Discover Weekly aren't just UX — they're strategic moats.

How to apply it

Ask: Does this feature increase engagement or retention? Are we optimising for the power user or the casual listener? How does this design decision affect our competitive position? Can AI make this experience uniquely ours?

Example

Spotify Wrapped turns user data into a viral marketing moment. It's a product design decision that drives growth — should they invest more in features that users share, or focus on core listening experience?
